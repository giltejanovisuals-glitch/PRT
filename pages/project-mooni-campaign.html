Mooni —
Customizing the
Sleep Experience

A multi-platform visual campaign translating Mooni's customizable mattress features into clear, calming, and lifestyle-led communication across social media, retail, and digital touchpoints.

Art Direction, Campaign Design, Photo Manipulation, Social Media
Industry
Sleep and Wellness
Year
2026

Project Overview

Mooni makes customizable mattresses, but their marketing didn't yet reflect how considered the product itself was. This campaign gave the brand a calm, editorial visual language built to travel across social, retail, and digital - without losing the premium, wellness-led feeling at the core of the product.

The Challenge

Mooni needed launch visuals that felt as calm and premium as the product itself, without the campaign reading as flat, generic, or over-produced. The brand's customizable features are genuinely differentiating, but hard to communicate visually without the campaign turning into a spec sheet.

Creative Direction

I moved away from harsh studio lighting toward soft, low-contrast photography and quiet, breathing layouts - letting sage greens and soft neutrals carry the mood instead of bold color blocking. Every layout keeps generous negative space, so the product and its customization story stay the focal point rather than competing with the design around them.

Campaign System

Product Education

Clear, calm breakdowns of Mooni's customizable layers and materials, built for product pages and in-depth social posts.

Lifestyle Storytelling

Editorial, bedroom-set photography framing Mooni as part of a considered daily routine, not just a mattress.

Promotional Content

Launch and seasonal campaign assets - key visuals, countdown content, and offer callouts - built on the same visual grammar.

Retail Applications

In-store signage, shelf talkers, and POS materials that carry the campaign's calm, editorial tone onto the shop floor.

Project Value

What the campaign delivered for Mooni:

  • 01 A single visual system that held together across social, retail, and digital, instead of separate one-off assets.
  • 02 A calmer, more premium brand feeling that better matched the product's own quality.
  • 03 A reusable layout and photography grammar the in-house team can extend to future product lines.
